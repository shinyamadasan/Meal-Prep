## 5 Most Influential Sources

### 1. SheetJS Stream Export API (official-docs, critical)
`external/2026-05-20-sheetjs-streaming-api.md`

**Why it matters:** Contains the single most important architectural finding: SheetJS CANNOT stream-read XLSX files. The ZIP container requires buffering the entire file before parsing. Any guide that implies SheetJS streaming reads are possible is wrong. This finding directly shapes `guides/01-streaming-parse-large-files.md` - the recommended pattern for large XLSX reads is Web Worker isolation (not streaming), with `xlsx-stream-rows` as the third-party alternative for true bounded-memory iteration.

### 2. OWASP CSV Injection (official-docs, critical)
`external/2026-05-20-owasp-csv-injection.md`

**Why it matters:** The canonical authoritative definition of CSV injection, including the complete list of dangerous characters (`=`, `+`, `-`, `@`, tab, CR, LF, full-width variants) and the critical finding that no universal sanitization strategy is safe. Combined with the empirical results from deadends.dev (tab-prefix = 90% survival), this pair forms the complete foundation for `guides/04-csv-injection-prevention.md`.

### 3. OneSchema vs Dromo Comparison 2026 (blog, critical)
`external/2026-05-20-oneschema-vs-dromo-comparison.md`

**Why it matters:** The most comprehensive 2026 comparison of the three managed importer services. This GDPR/HIPAA implication is often overlooked and must be called out in `guides/02-column-mapping-ux.md`. Also confirms Dromo's $499/month entry price and OneSchema's lack of a free tier.

## Open Questions Answered by Research

- **Q1: Is react-spreadsheet-import mature enough in 2026?**
  YES. v4.7.1, last release August 2024, MIT licensed, actively maintained. Chakra UI dependency is worth noting. `bow-react-spreadsheet-import` fork exists for teams wanting to avoid Chakra. Recommend RSI as the default self-hosted option.

- **Q2: SheetJS licensing status 2026?**
  Apache 2.0 Community Edition remains free for commercial use. Attribution required in OSS disclosures. No licensing change announced for 2026. Pro edition exists for advanced features but is not required for standard CSV/XLSX parse workflows.

- **Q3: Free tier for OneSchema or Flatfile?**
  Flatfile YES - 50 files/month + 10M PDV free, no credit card required. OneSchema NO - no free tier, requires sales call, annual commitment, ~$38,650/year median contract.

- **Q4: ExcelJS vs xlsx-populate for server-side generation?**
  ExcelJS wins for generating styled reports from scratch. xlsx-populate is only relevant for modifying existing Excel template files (mail-merge style). ExcelJS has a known memory leak in WorkbookWriter (Issue #2916) pending fix in v5.0.
