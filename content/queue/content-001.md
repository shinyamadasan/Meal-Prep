I'm building an app almost entirely by directing AI agents from my phone. Last month, one of them deleted a folder of another AI's work. No trash bin. No undo. Here's what happened — and the one-line rule that fixed it. 🧵

---

My app isn't built by one AI. Several work on it side by side — one plans, one writes code, others keep their own notes running quietly in the background. A small team, not one brain.

---

One day, while tidying up, one assistant found a folder it didn't recognize. No context attached. Just... unfamiliar. Looked like clutter.

---

It deleted it.

---

It wasn't clutter. It was another AI's active work — and because it had never been backed up into the project's saved history, it wasn't a "restore from trash" delete. It was just gone.

---

I had to notice, explain what had actually been there, and get it rebuilt by hand.

---

Nobody was malicious. The mistake was confidence: "I don't recognize this, so it's probably safe to remove." That's exactly backwards.

---

The fix wasn't a smarter algorithm. It was the same rule you'd give a new hire on day one: if you don't know what something is, you ask before you touch it.

---

That rule has held ever since. Building with AI agents is incredible — but "confident" and "right" are not the same thing, and the gap between them is exactly where you need a rule like this.
